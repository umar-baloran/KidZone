Multiplication

The basic idea of multiplication is repeated addition.
For example: 5 x 3 = 5 + 5 + 5 = 15
But as well as multiplying by whole numbers, you can also multiply by fractions or decimals.
For example 5 x 3(1/2) = 5 + 5 + 5 + (half of 5) = 17.5



Your life will be a lot easier when you can simply remember the multiplication tables.

Tip 1: Order Does Not Matter


When we multiply two numbers, it does not matter which is first or second, the answer is always the same.

Example:
3x5=15, and 5x3=15



In fact half of the table is a mirror image of the other!
So, don't memorize both "3x5" and "5x3", just memorize that "a 3 and a 5 make 15" when multiplied.
