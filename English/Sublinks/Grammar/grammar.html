Grammar

Noun

A noun is the name of a person, place, thing, or idea. Whatever exists, we assume, can be named, and that name is a noun. Here are some examples of a person, place and thing, person: man, woman, teacher, John, Mary. place: home, office, town, countryside, America. thing: table, car, banana, money, music, love, dog, monkey.

Examples of noun:
1.Girls in white dresses with blue satin sashes.
2.When the dog bites.

Verb

A verb is a word that expresses action or state of a being. Here are some examples of a action and state of a being, A physical action (e.g., to swim, to write, to climb). A mental action (e.g., to think, to guess, to consider). A state of being (e.g., to be, to exist, to appear).

Examples of verb:
1.Alison bought a ticket.
2.She considers the job done

Adjectives

The simplest definition of an adjective is that it is a word that describes or clarifies a noun. Adjectives describe nouns by giving some information about an object’s size, shape, age, color, origin or material.

Examples of Adjectives:
1.It’s an old table.
2.Follow the yellow cab.

Pronoun

A pronoun is a word that takes the place of or refers to a noun.


Examples of Pronoun:
1.He passed the test.
2.Please give it to her.

Adverb

An adverb can be added to a verb to modify its meaning.
Usually,an adverb tells you when, where, how, in what manner, or to what extent an action is performed. Many adverbs end in ly and many adverbs do not end in ly.

Examples of Adverb:
1.Anita placed the vase carefully on the shelf.
2.The dinner guests arrived early.
